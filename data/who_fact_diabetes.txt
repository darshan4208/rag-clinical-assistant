Title: WHO Diabetes Fact Sheet (2023)  
Source: https://www.who.int/news-room/fact-sheets/detail/diabetes  

Key Facts:
- Approximately 422 million people globally have diabetes.  
- Type 2 diabetes is the most common, largely the result of excess body weight and physical inactivity.  
- It is a major cause of blindness, kidney failure, heart attacks, and lower limb amputation.  

Prevention & Control:
- Healthy diet, regular physical activity, and avoiding tobacco can prevent or delay type 2 diabetes.
- Diagnosis and treatment should include blood glucose control and addressing cardiovascular risk.
